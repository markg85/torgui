Do note, all traffic is encrypted! But because of this a private key needs to be known too (you need to encrypt a message with your private key and the server's public key). This is asymmetric encryption. The NKN address that you need to specify here is the address of the server that handles your requests. It gets all the data in an encrypted way. To do that, it needs to know your public key (that is your wallet address), but to complete the circle we here need to know your private key (the seed key) which is used to open your wallet and send a message to the given NKN address in your name. If you don't trust this, just make a new NKN wallet and use that for this site.

Neither the seed key nor the NKN address leaves your browser. They are stored in the browsers local storage.
